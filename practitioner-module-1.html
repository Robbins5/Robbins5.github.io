Practitioner Module 1

Foundations: What AI Is — and Why Internal Audit Must Understand It

This module establishes foundational AI literacy for audit professionals. It defines the core terminology, explains how AI systems operate architecturally, and translates those mechanics into assurance implications.

1. Why This Matters Now

Artificial intelligence is embedded across enterprise systems — from productivity platforms to ERP workflows, fraud detection models, and agent-driven automation. The relevant question for internal audit is not whether AI is coming, but where it is already operating and how it changes accountability, evidence, control design, and assurance expectations.

When AI becomes part of the operating model, assurance must evolve alongside it.

2. Core Terminology Audit Professionals Must Understand

Generative AI

Systems that produce content — text, code, analysis — in response to prompts. Output may influence documentation, reporting, or operational workflows.

Large Language Models (LLMs)

Statistical models trained on massive datasets. They generate probable language patterns — not verified truth. Output must be reviewed and validated.

Machine Learning (ML)

Pattern-detection systems trained on structured data for anomaly detection, forecasting, and classification. Introduces model validation and drift risk.

AI Agents

Systems capable of taking action — retrieving data, triggering workflows, initiating tasks. When AI acts, not just advises, control design becomes more complex.

Embedded AI

AI functionality built directly into enterprise systems. May not be visibly labeled as AI, yet influences decisions and control reliance.

3. How AI Actually Works (From an Assurance Perspective)

Most AI systems follow this lifecycle:

  • Data input
  • Model processing
  • Output generation
  • Human or system action

Data

What feeds the model? Is the data controlled? Is it sensitive?

Model

Who selected it? Was it validated? How is drift monitored?

Output

Is output reviewed? Logged? Challenged?

Action

What is automated? Who is accountable for outcomes?

4. What Is Trending Inside Enterprises

  • Copilot integration in productivity tools
  • AI-assisted coding environments
  • Embedded AI in ERP and SaaS platforms
  • Agent-driven automation
  • Retrieval-augmented generation pulling internal data

5. The Quiet Risk: Informal AI Use

Employees may use AI externally to draft analysis, summarize policies, or inform judgment. Even informal usage influences decision-making and therefore affects risk posture.

6. Key Takeaways for Audit Professionals

  • AI systems are probabilistic, not deterministic.
  • Output quality depends on prompt and data context.
  • Embedded AI may already affect controls.
  • Agents introduce action-level risk.
  • Evidence influenced by AI requires validation.
  • Governance must go beyond tool approval.
